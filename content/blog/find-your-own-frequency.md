**You can't fix a broken marketing funnel by adding another Javascript tag, no that's not that simple.**

What I believe was completely wrong.

Yes, I was one of them, a jack-of-all-trades, an expert in every area of digital marketing, a classic mistake that many people make unintentionally, that was a few years ago.

Managing Meta ads, configuring WordPress sites, trying to piece client-side pixels together to see an improvement on the dashboard, I was there.

The freelance system is ruthless. It actively pushes you to become a “jack-of-all-trades.” Clients want you to handle SEO, copywriting, design, and analytics for a flat fee. The market consistently rewards cheap, fragile structures over real, solid architecture. It’s exhausting.

The freelance system was pushing you to become a swiss knife, it still does. Clients have higher expectation for a flat fee, they want you to take care of SEO, copywriting, design and analytics.
I was tired to see that market consistently rewards cheap and clone structures over original and solid architectures.

Somehow, I managed to come across the work of engineers like Simo Ahava. I told myself : "Joseph, this man doesn't just add tags to a website, what's doing is something else, he's designing data pipelines and approaching tracking as a software architecture while focusing heavily on server-side logic and data governance , but How ? ."

This shift and that How question in perspective completely shaped my career.
I made a decision to stopped trying to do everything, because it was no good and started to focus entirely on the backend. 

I no longer touch superficial marketing. I build server-side monitoring infrastructures. 
Using Google Cloud Platform, Node.js, and BigQuery.
I try my best to route clean, consistent data signals directly to algorithms and analytics dashboards, also open to work with server-side tagging easy to implement premium systems like Addingwell, Stape, Usercentrics, etc. However, my goal is still shifting myself from a service provider setup person to a genuine system builder. 

In short, the freelance market can be tough, and the tech world changes very quickly. 
However, once you find that frequency to stop being a jack-of-all-trades and start building actual systems, you’re no longer competing on price.
You become the architect who stops revenue loss. 
_And that’s quite a solid reason to keep developing._
