Do you feel the pinch when it comes to your marketing budget disappearing into a black hole recently? 

If you’re an entrepreneur like me, then you've probably realized the shift on what it takes to gain a new client; it feels more challenging to do so now compared to before. And that’s not just an impression. 

B2B Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC) have increased over 60% since 2021 due to the increase in cost of advertising, more extended periods between when sales start and finish, and increased competition. 

The traditional way of doing business has just turned into spending money quickly.

The true "invisible" issue is that 40% to 60% of your conversions (i.e., customers who convert) are missing from your reports. 

Nowadays many businesses no matter the field use traditional "client-side" tracking methods, such as tracking pixels, which have been severely impacted by the privacy restrictions from iOS Safari and blocking with advertising. Losing half your data as a solopreneur is not just a reporting problem; it is a complete strategic failure because the AI algorithms are unable to retrieve their correct audience data from your information.

     - Transforming my perspective: From "Client-Side" to "Server-Side": I'm moving towards implementing server-to-server tracking methods (such as Meta CAPI or Google Enhanced Conversions). This change is not simply an upgrade of technologies but also includes the issue of data sovereignty, and it allows me to gain an accurate view of my true ROI by avoiding the limitations and data barricades of browsers.

    - From Volume to Value: I no longer tell the ad platforms that I'm using, "Hey, go and find me any lead." Instead of that, now I'm using Value-Based Bidding, where I give greater importance to what I consider to be of true value (e.g., a "booked demo") versus something of significantly less value (e.g., a "newsletter signup"). 

By properly training the algorithm regarding the true worth of my customers, I am able to maximize my budget for profit rather than simply for vanity metrics.

    - Bringing the Data to Life: Each "Conversion" represents a consumer, not just a number. The ability to take ownership of my data allows me to create enjoyable, lasting memories and manageable experiences for my customers. First-party data also helps me to treat my audience as individuals instead of just unknown hashes.

We have to work efficiently and intelligently if we want to survive in this ecosystem, because the future has changed drastically over the years. You don’t necessarily need the largest budget; what you really need is accurate data.

As an entrepreneur, my long-term focus is on achieving sustainable growth rather than "burning the cash" with my clients.
